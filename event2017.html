Events-2017



  • Talk: Network Design with Parametric Uncertainties

    • After 70 years of Independence, India has by virtue of skilled engineering and virtuous leaderships, developed an infrastructure that springs the nation as one of the strong economies in the modern world. However inherently marred with the challenges of scale and the consequent uncertainty in systems results in extreme bottlenecks and thus failure. With said infrastructure in place, an immediate approach to address aforementioned (decision) problems is to find optimal solutions (often discrete in nature) within the context of concerned systems. These solutions must be robust to both the problem scale and parametric uncertainties, often the cause of failure for any technology or operational system, ever so evident in this particular case. This necessity becomes further important in the context of the Himalayan region, that enjoys both the natural resources and the accompanying geographical challenges. In this talk, Dr Bharadwaj had discussed both the aforementioned issues : how to model uncertainty in robust optimization problems and furthermore how to find fast exact optimal solutions utilising geometrical and combinatorial structure of the problem in context. In particular,the applications using a framework that can be used to model several real life problems.
    Date: 18/12/2017 Speaker:Dr. Avinash Bhardwaj from Center for Operations Research and Econometrics, UC Louvain, Belgium

  • Talk: :Environmental Sustainability – Myth OR Reality?

    • Pollution from anthropogenic sources has led to widespread environmental degradation. Environmental sustainability is threatened due issues such as global warming, ozone layer depletion, spread of toxic chemicals as well as water and air pollution.  Development, which is environmentally unsustainable, has potential to threaten the very existence of the human race on this planet earth. As a result, man must learn to live in an environmentally sustainable way in order to limit the widespread damage to environment.
    Date: 14/12/2016 Speaker:Prof. Ajit P. Annachhatre
